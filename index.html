A word from Yu-Han

“I’m a Taiwanese artist currently living in Paris. I was born in Kaohsiung, Taiwan, just before the democratic transition. I received an education in taiwanese traditional art technique and culture. After completing my studies, I went to Spain to attend some art classes in Valencia University and participate in art exhibitions. In my artwork, I use the notion of "Change", as defined by Tao philosophy. My creation has been strongly influenced by Laozi’s philosophy: his book Tao Te Ching guided my conception of art. Tao explains that the universe is always changing; the only thing that cannot change is change itself. I’m using painting to investigate what is actually this “change”. As I was painting about nature, I found out that “air” belongs to Tao. For me, “air” is one expression of Tao but it’s invisible, so technically, “air” is abstract. In my work, air blurs the outlines of figurative art or covers some figurative elements. The function of “air” for me is to give my work more vitality and harmony, and make it more changeable”.

About The Laguna Art Gallery

Founded by Adasme Brothers in 2015 and focused on international contemporary art, The Laguna Gallery has been developed as a platform for discovery and promotion of both young and established artists only through digital media. The Laguna Art Gallery is based in Paris, France.
